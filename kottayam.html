Kottayam

Kottayam, situated in the heart of Kerala, is a district known for its cultural richness, religious diversity, and lush landscapes. The district is often referred to as the "Land of Letters" due to its significant contributions to literature and education. It houses the Mahatma Gandhi University and the CMS College, one of the oldest colleges in India, reflecting Kottayam's prominence in the field of education.

In addition to its academic prowess, Kottayam is surrounded by picturesque landscapes, including the famous Vembanad Lake and the backwaters of Kumarakom. The region is renowned for its rubber plantations, contributing substantially to Kerala's rubber industry. Pilgrim centers like the Ettumanoor Mahadeva Temple and the St. Mary's Church at Bharananganam add to the district's cultural tapestry. Kottayam's unique combination of intellectual pursuits, natural beauty, and religious heritage makes it a captivating destination in the God's Own Country.
