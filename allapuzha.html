Alappuzha

Alappuzha, often referred to as the "Venice of the East," is a mesmerizing district in Kerala renowned for its intricate network of backwaters, lagoons, and canals. The district's crown jewel is the Vembanad Lake, the longest lake in India, offering enchanting houseboat cruises that showcase the serene beauty of the region. The annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race, held in Punnamada Lake, adds a burst of excitement with vibrant snake boat races that attract spectators from far and wide.

Apart from its aquatic allure, Alappuzha is steeped in cultural richness. The city's colonial architecture, including the Alappuzha Lighthouse and historic buildings, reflects its historical significance as a major trading post. The Alappuzha Beach, with its golden sands and picturesque sunsets, is a popular spot for leisure and relaxation. With its unique blend of natural splendor and cultural heritage, Alappuzha stands as a captivating destination that allows visitors to immerse themselves in the tranquil beauty of Kerala's backwaters.
