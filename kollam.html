Kollam

Kollam, situated along the picturesque shores of the Arabian Sea, is a district in Kerala known for its unique blend of historical significance and natural beauty. The city boasts the Ashtamudi Lake, the second-largest in the state, famous for its tranquil backwaters and vibrant houseboat cruises. Kollam has been a prominent trading hub since ancient times, recognized for its cashew processing industry and historic Kollam Port, which has played a crucial role in maritime trade.

The district is not only a haven for nature enthusiasts but also holds cultural importance with landmarks like the Thangassery Light House and the Portuguese-built churches. The annual Kollam Pooram festival adds a splash of color and fervor to the city, showcasing traditional art forms and grand processions. Kollam's unique charm lies in its ability to seamlessly blend history, commerce, and natural beauty, making it a captivating destination on Kerala's southwestern coast.
