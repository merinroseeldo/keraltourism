Idduki

Idukki, situated in the high ranges of Kerala, is a district renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, dense forests, and extensive wildlife sanctuaries. The district is home to the iconic Idukki Dam, one of the largest arch dams in Asia, which not only contributes significantly to hydroelectric power generation but also stands as a marvel of engineering amidst the scenic Western Ghats.

Idukki's terrain is characterized by hills, valleys, and lush greenery, offering a haven for nature enthusiasts. The district hosts numerous wildlife sanctuaries, including the Idukki Wildlife Sanctuary, where visitors can encounter diverse flora and fauna. The hill stations of Munnar and Vagamon, known for their tea plantations and mist-covered mountains, add to the district's charm. Idukki is not only a reservoir of natural beauty but also a vital part of Kerala's hydroelectric power generation, making it a unique and essential district in the state.
